我們現在把問題分成兩個層面：

[  
\text{信息論／熵層}  
]

和

[  
\text{素數分佈層}.  
]

這很重要。若熵層本身只是抽象計數，那它不危險；真正危險的是它要求素數 residues 滿足過強隨機性，例如「所有短區間中都完全均勻」。我們要避免把路線推到這種鐵壁上。

---

## 1. 熵層真正想證什麼？

令

# [  
\mathcal A_R

\left{  
a=(a_p)_{p\in P}:  
Q(a)\le R  
\right}  
]

是低能量 assignments。這裏

[  
Q(a)=  
\sum_{pq\in E_{\mathrm{test}}}  
|\phi_{pq}(a)|^2.  
]

令 (A) 是在 (\mathcal A_R) 上均勻隨機的 assignment。熵為

[  
H(A)=\log |\mathcal A_R|.  
]

我們想證：

[  
H(A)  
]

很小，除非 (A) 基本上是由一個全局小整數 (h) 決定，也就是主弧。

換句話說：

[  
\boxed{  
\text{低能量集合的熵小。}  
}  
]

這是純信息論目標。

---

## 2. 邊約束如何降低熵？

對一條測試邊 (pq)，低能量意味

# [  
\phi_{pq}(A)

\frac{A_p q^{-1}}p+  
\frac{A_q p^{-1}}q  
]

常常接近整數。

若 (A_q) 已知，則這條邊把 (A_p) 限制在一個短模區間中。若 (p) 有多個測試鄰居 (q_1,\dots,q_t)，則

[  
A_p  
]

受到多個模區間限制。因此條件熵

[  
H(A_p\mid A_{q_1},\dots,A_{q_t})  
]

應顯著小於

[  
\log p.  
]

理想情況下：

[  
H(A_p\mid A_{T(p)})  
\le \varepsilon\log p + O(R_p),  
]

其中 (T(p)) 是 (p) 的測試鄰居，(R_p) 是 (p) 附近的能量消耗。

這就是 local entropy contraction。

---

## 3. 這裏需要什麼素數分佈？

只需要測試斜率

[  
q^{-1}\pmod p  
]

不要全部落在壞位置。更精確地，我們需要：對每個 (p)，可找到約

[  
C\log p  
]

個鄰居 (q)，使得對 (A_p) 的模區間測試足夠獨立。

這不要求「所有素數完美隨機」。只要求在某個可控上層區間中，prime residues mod (p) 足夠分散，能提供 (O(\log p)) 個好測試斜率。

這比要求全局均勻弱很多。

我們可以選

[  
q\asymp p^{3/2},  
]

這樣 interval 長度遠大於 (p)，素數在模 (p) 的 residue 分佈相對容易處理；也在 Irving 可控範圍邊界內。這避免了最危險的「短區間 primes mod large modulus」問題。

所以目前並未撞上不可動的素數鐵壁。

---

## 4. 信息論全局拼接

若對多數頂點有

[  
H(A_p\mid A_{T(p)})  
\le \varepsilon\log p+O(R_p),  
]

如何推出整體

[  
H(A)  
]

小？

這需要一個 entropy covering inequality。粗略地，如果測試圖有良好 expansion，則全局熵不能遠大於局部條件熵總和。這類思想在 Shearer inequality、agreement testing、local-to-global entropy arguments 中常見。

形式上希望得到：

[  
H(A)  
\le  
C\sum_p H(A_p\mid A_{T(p)})  
+  
\text{small seed entropy}.  
]

若測試圖沒有自由大種子，則 seed entropy 可控。這就是我們為什麼需要雙向／擴張測試圖，而不是單向上升圖。

單向圖會留下最高層自由熵；雙向 expander 才能閉合。

---

## 5. 可能的熱力學圖像

你的「熱力學」直覺其實很準。可以這樣看：

- 每個 assignment (a) 是一個微觀狀態；
    
- 能量是
    
    [  
    Q(a)=\sum_{e}|\phi_e(a)|^2;  
    ]
    
- Fourier 係數貢獻類似 Boltzmann weight
    
    [  
    e^{-cQ(a)};  
    ]
    
- 我們要證 partition function
    
    [  
    Z=\sum_{a\notin\mathfrak M}e^{-cQ(a)}  
    ]
    
    很小。
    

主弧是低能基態族；agreement theorem 說：低能態只能靠近主弧；其他態雖然數量大，但能量也大，總 Boltzmann 權重小。

這個視角非常自然，而且比逐個 (h) 分類更正確。

---

## 6. 可能的鐵壁在哪裏？

目前可能的鐵壁有三個：

### A. Prime inverse slopes 不夠隨機

若無條件無法保證 (q^{-1}\bmod p) 提供足夠測試斜率，就會卡在素數分佈。

但因我們可選 (q\sim p^{3/2})，這看起來不是最嚴重。應有 Bombieri–Vinogradov / large sieve / Irving 平均估計可支撐。

### B. Entropy contraction 不夠強

局部條件熵可能只能降到 (0.9\log p)，而不是足夠小。那全局仍有巨大 entropy。

但每個頂點可取 (C\log p) 條測試邊，(C) 可大；只要測試斜率有基本獨立性，就可任意增強 contraction。這也不太像鐵壁。

### C. Agreement propagation 失敗

即使局部 lists 小，可能仍有 exponentially many globally consistent assignments，不來自單一 (h)。

這是最真實的風險。它相當於存在許多「低能玻璃態」：每條邊局部相容，但全局不對角。若有這種玻璃態，Fourier 路線會很難。

測試圖的 expansion 和 CRT rigidity 旨在排除它。這是目前核心。

---

## 7. 如何檢查 C？

我們可以看 exact zero-energy 情形。

若

[  
\phi_{pq}(a)=0  
]

對所有測試邊成立，那麼對每條邊 (pq)，CRT glue (H_{pq}=0)，因此

[  
a_p=0,\qquad a_q=0.  
]

若圖連通，立刻所有

[  
a_p=0.  
]

所以 exact ground state 唯一，沒有玻璃態。

對 small but nonzero energy，應該只有 small global (h) 的近似態：

[  
a_p\equiv h\pmod p,\qquad |h|\text{ small}.  
]

這是很強的正面訊號：零能剛性非常強。低能玻璃態若存在，必須是近似效應，不是精確代數自由度。這讓 agreement theorem 更可信。

---

## 8. 小 (h) 為何是低能態？

若

[  
a_p\equiv h\pmod p,  
]

則

[  
\phi_{pq}(a)\equiv \frac{h}{pq}.  
]

若所有 (pq) 都大於 (|h|)，則相位很小，能量約

[  
h^2\sum_{pq}\frac1{p^2q^2}.  
]

這就是 Gaussian 主弧。它是合法低能族。

Agreement theorem 應該說：低能態基本都來自這種 (h)。

這非常像 Poincaré inequality：函數在邊上變化小，則接近常數。只不過這裏「常數」是 CRT global integer (h)，邊差是 CRT glue interval。

這個類比很好：

[  
\boxed{  
\text{CRT agreement theorem 是一種 arithmetic Poincaré inequality.}  
}  
]

---

## 9. 這給下一步的證明策略

不要從 lists 開始，而從 Poincaré 型不等式開始：

想證：

[  
Q(a)  
\ge  
c\cdot \operatorname{dist}(a,\mathfrak M)^2  
]

某種意義下，(\mathfrak M) 是對角小整數族。

如果能建立這種 spectral gap / Poincaré inequality，entropy 控制會自然跟上。

在 exact setting，edge energy 強迫端點為零；在 approximate setting，要量化。

這可能比 list agreement 更分析化，也更接近圖 Laplacian。

## 10. 可能的線性化

對邊 (pq)，CRT glue symmetric representative (H_{pq}^{*}) 是滿足

[  
H_{pq}^{_}\equiv a_p\pmod p,\quad  
H_{pq}^{_}\equiv a_q\pmod q  
]

的最小大小整數。

低能量表示

[  
H_{pq}^{*}  
]

通常很小。

若 (H_{pq}^{*}) 小於 (\min(p,q))，則必有

[  
a_p=a_q=H_{pq}^{*}  
]

as ordinary integers, provided residues represented in small range. Thus for very low edge energy, endpoints literally equal a small integer.

For moderate low energy, (H_{pq}^{*}) may exceed p or q, but if (\delta) chosen small and scales not too skewed, still imposes strong agreement.

This suggests proving agreement first at a threshold where

[  
|H_{pq}^{*}|<\min(p,q)  
]

for many edges. Since our connected scales have ratio up to (p^{1/2}), (pq) much larger than min. Condition (\delta pq<\min(p,q)) would require (\delta<1/\max(p,q)), impossible fixed. So not enough.

But energy small in square average may force many edges much smaller than fixed (\delta pq), perhaps down to min scale? Not necessarily. Main arc h can be as large as (1/\sigma\sim k_0 2^{k_0}), while min p~2^{k0}; h can exceed p by factor k0. So literal equality too strong.

Still, low energy gives (H_{pq}^{*}) of size comparable to common h, not to pq. Need recover h.

---

## 11. A possible reconstruction of h

For a path (p_0,p_1,\dots,p_m), if each edge has small CRT glue (H_i), then (a_{p_i}\equiv H_i\pmod{p_i}) and (a_{p_{i+1}}\equiv H_i\pmod{p_{i+1}}). Consecutive edges sharing vertex (p_i) imply

[  
H_{i-1}\equiv H_i\pmod{p_i}.  
]

If both (H_{i-1},H_i) are not too large compared with (p_i), then they must be equal. Thus along paths through sufficiently large intermediate primes, edge glues become equal to a common h.

This is excellent.

For main arc h up to (H_0=1/\sigma\sim k_0 2^{k_0}), if we use intermediate primes (p_i) much larger than (H_0), equality propagates. We can choose graph layers with primes much larger than major arc threshold? The smallest primes set determines (H_0). Higher layers are indeed larger.

Problem near bottom layer: (p\sim2^{k0}) may be smaller than (H_0). But after one edge to upper layer (q\sim p^{3/2}), q may exceed H0 if (p^{3/2}\gg k0 p), true for large p. Thus upper layers can propagate equality.

This is very promising.

---

## 12. Path propagation strategy

1. Use low energy to show many edge glues (H_{pq}) are bounded by some (B), say (B\sim C/\sigma), the major arc scale.
    
2. Choose test graph so every vertex connects to higher-scale vertices (q>B).
    
3. If two good edges share such a high vertex (q>B), their glues are congruent mod q and both have absolute value <B<q, so they are equal as integers.
    
4. Therefore good edges incident to a high vertex share a common glue value.
    
5. Expansion propagates this common value across graph, yielding a global h.
    

This bypasses much entropy/list complexity! It uses scale separation.

This is a big simplification.

---

## 13. Check feasibility

Major arc scale

[  
B\sim 1/\sigma\sim k_0 2^{k_0}.  
]

For a bottom vertex p~2^{k0}, upper neighbor q~p^{3/2}=2^{1.5k0}. For large k0,

[  
q\gg k_0 2^{k_0}=B.  
]

So upper vertices are larger than B. Good.

If all low-energy edge glues satisfy (|H_e|<B), then at high vertices equality propagates.

But low energy only gives square sum small, not every edge. Edges with (|H_e|>B) contribute at least ((B/(pq))^2), which may be tiny for huge pq. Need threshold relative to pq. Wait energy term is (|H_e|^2/(pq)^2). If pq huge, H_e could exceed B with small relative energy. So low energy does not bound absolute glue by B on high-scale edges.

For main arc h, H_e=h for all edges, so absolute B. But a bad assignment might have H_e growing with pq yet relative small. Example H_e=δ pq gives fixed energy per edge, not low if many edges. If total energy R small compared to number edges, most edges have small relative glue, but absolute may be huge on high pq.

To propagate equality via high vertex q, need H_e<q, i.e. relative glue <1/p. For edge pq, condition (|H_e|/(pq)<1/p) means phase <1/p. Low energy average may not give that; typical low phase threshold is maybe (\sqrt{R/m}), not as small as 1/p.

So path propagation by absolute bound may fail unless energy extremely low.

But for large Fourier coefficient, energy R may be O(1) while number edges huge, so average phase squared tiny. Could be below 1/p^2? Need compare.

For edge around p,q with q~p^{3/2}, number of such edges per p ~C log p in test graph. Energy low R distributed over ~log L test edges. If R=O(log major arc), average per edge maybe not tiny enough. Not reliable.

So not full solution, but useful for very low energy.

---

## 14. Hybrid

- Very low energy: path propagation by absolute small glues gives global h.
    
- Moderate low energy: entropy/list decoding handles.
    

This is acceptable. It is a natural energy stratification, not arbitrary case split.

We can define:  
[  
B_e=\min(p,q)  
]  
absolute agreement threshold. Edges with (|H_e|<B_e/2) force literal equality of glue at shared large vertices. If many such edges, agreement propagation easy.  
If not, many edges have (|H_e|\ge B_e/2), contributing energy (\ge 1/\max(p,q)^2). Summed over many test edges, gives energy cost. Maybe enough to pay entropy.

This resembles high/low temperature decomposition.

---

## 15. Current best picture

The entropy/thermal language is not misleading; it is the right way. But we can avoid relying on mysterious prime randomness by using:

1. scale separation for very low energy;
    
2. local list entropy for moderate energy;
    
3. Irving for reciprocal slope distribution;
    
4. weighted local CLT for主弧.
    

This is still complex, but not hitting a single prime-distribution iron wall yet.

## 16. Next step

I would next formalize the **energy-stratified agreement theorem**:

For a weighted test graph, low-energy assignments satisfy either:

- they are close to a diagonal (h), or
    
- they pay entropy-proportional energy.
    

This is exactly what Fourier sum needs.

In formula:

[  
\log N(R)  
\le  
\log N_{\mathrm{diag}}(R)+C R,  
]

where (N(R)=|{a:Q(a)\le R}|).

This is the cleanest target. Then all preceding tools become ingredients to prove this inequality.

So next I will write this inequality and see how it plugs directly into the Fourier partition function.
