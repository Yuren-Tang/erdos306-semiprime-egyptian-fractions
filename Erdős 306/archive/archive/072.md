好。現在分析新的原子 gadget：

[  
P \longleftrightarrow Q  
]

其中 (P) 是低層素數塊，

[  
p\in P,\qquad p\asymp X,  
]

而 (Q) 是高層素數塊，

[  
q\in Q,\qquad q\asymp X^{3/2}.  
]

關鍵是：

[  
q\gg H_{\mathrm{maj}},  
]

其中

[  
H_{\mathrm{maj}}\asymp 1/\sigma  
]

是主弧 (h) 的自然大小。高層頂點 (q) 因為模數大，可以比較不同低層邊的 CRT glue 是否真的是同一個小整數。

---

## 1. 高層比較器的基本機制

對邊 (pq)，令

[  
H_{pq}^{*}\in(-pq/2,pq/2]  
]

是 CRT glue：

[  
H_{pq}^{_}\equiv a_p\pmod p,\qquad  
H_{pq}^{_}\equiv b_q\pmod q.  
]

若兩條邊

[  
p_1q,\qquad p_2q  
]

都 incident to 同一個高層頂點 (q)，則

[  
H_{p_1q}^{_}\equiv H_{p_2q}^{_}\pmod q,  
]

因為二者都同餘於 (b_q\pmod q)。

若同時

[  
|H_{p_1q}^{_}|<q/2,\qquad |H_{p_2q}^{_}|<q/2,  
]

則必有

[  
H_{p_1q}^{_}=H_{p_2q}^{_}  
]

作為普通整數。

所以高層頂點 (q) 有一個比較功能：

[  
\boxed{  
\text{所有 incident 的「絕對小」good edges 必須共享同一個 glue }h_q.  
}  
]

這正是 agreement test 裏「局部視圖重疊處一致」的角色。

---

## 2. 主弧為何能通過比較器？

若 assignment 來自全局 (h)：

[  
a_p\equiv h\pmod p,\qquad b_q\equiv h\pmod q,  
]

且

[  
|h|<q/2,  
]

則每條邊

[  
H_{pq}^{*}=h.  
]

因此同一個高層 (q) 的所有 incident glues 自然相等。主弧完全通過。

這也是我們選高層 (q\gg H_{\mathrm{maj}}) 的原因：要讓主弧 (h) 在高層比較器中被視為「絕對小」，從而能傳播一致性。

---

## 3. 若不是同一個 (h)，會發生什麼？

假設某些低層頂點 (p) 試圖使用不同的局部 (h_p)。若它們連到同一個高層 (q)，並且邊 glue 都絕對小，則高層比較器迫使

[  
h_{p_1}=h_{p_2}.  
]

所以不同局部 (h) 的低層頂點不能共同大量連到同一批高層頂點而保持低能。

在一張二部 expander 中，若低層被分成多個不同 (h)-cluster，跨 cluster 會在高層產生衝突。要避免衝突，只能讓許多邊變成「非絕對小」，這些邊付能量。

因此：

[  
\boxed{  
\text{多個局部 }h\text{ 的 coexistence 會產生 energy cost proportional to expansion boundary.}  
}  
]

這是我們要的 Poincaré 機制。

---

## 4. 如何定義「絕對小」good edge？

選一個 cutoff

[  
B  
]

略大於主弧範圍：

[  
B\asymp C/\sigma.  
]

稱邊 (pq) 為 **strongly good** 若

[  
|H_{pq}^{*}|\le B.  
]

若高層 (q>B)，則同一 (q) 上所有 strongly good 邊有相同 glue。

稱邊為 **weak/bad** 若

[  
|H_{pq}^{*}|>B.  
]

這條邊的能量至少

[  
\left(\frac{B}{pq}\right)^2.  
]

所以如果某 assignment 有很多 bad edges，它就支付能量。

若能量低，則多數測試邊 strong good；高層比較器便迫使大範圍 glue 一致。

---

## 5. 能量支付是否足夠？

這裏要小心。對高層邊

[  
p\asymp X,\quad q\asymp X^{3/2},  
]

有

[  
pq\asymp X^{5/2}.  
]

一條 bad edge 的能量下界是

[  
\left(\frac{B}{X^{5/2}}\right)^2.  
]

若

[  
B\asymp 1/\sigma  
]

而 (\sigma) 由最低尺度決定，這個能量可能很小。單條 bad edge 貢獻微弱。

但是測試邊數很多，並且 Fourier 衰減來自總和。只要 bad edges 數量與可用邊數成比例，總能量仍可觀。

所以 agreement theorem 應該是加權的：不是按邊數，而是按

[  
\left(\frac{B}{pq}\right)^2  
]

加權計算 bad boundary。這和 Fourier 能量自然一致。

---

## 6. 高層比較器給出 cluster structure

在 strong-good 子圖中，每個高層頂點 (q) 定義一個 glue label

[  
h_q.  
]

若低層頂點 (p) 連到兩個 high vertices (q_1,q_2) 且兩條邊都 strong good，則

[  
H_{pq_1}^{_}\equiv H_{pq_2}^{_}\pmod p.  
]

也就是

[  
h_{q_1}\equiv h_{q_2}\pmod p.  
]

如果

[  
|h_{q_1}|,|h_{q_2}|\le B  
]

且

[  
p>2B,  
]

則

[  
h_{q_1}=h_{q_2}.  
]

但低層 (p\asymp X) 可能小於 (B)。這是問題。

解法：不要要求低層比較；用更高層繼續比較。也就是多尺度：

[  
P_k\to P_{3k/2}\to P_{(3/2)^2k}\to\cdots  
]

一旦模數超過 (B)，shared vertices enforce equality as ordinary integers. Then equality propagates downward by congruence plus multiple high neighbors.

所以多尺度 tower 本來就必要。

---

## 7. 主弧範圍與尺度塔

若最低尺度是

[  
X_0=2^{k_0},  
]

主弧 cutoff 約

[  
B\asymp k_0X_0.  
]

一層上升給

[  
X_1=X_0^{3/2}.  
]

對大 (X_0),

[  
X_1\gg k_0X_0=B.  
]

所以只需一層上升，高層已超過主弧 cutoff。

這很好。底層不能直接比較小 (h)，但上層可以。

因此 gadget

[  
P_{k_0}\leftrightarrow P_{1.5k_0}  
]

已足以比較底層 local h，只要底層頂點共享高層比較器。

---

## 8. 強 good graph 的 propagation

令 strong-good 子圖包含邊 (pq) with

[  
|H_{pq}^{*}|\le B.  
]

在高層 (Q) 中，每個 (q) 的 incident strong-good edges share label (h_q).

若兩個高層頂點 (q_1,q_2) share a low neighbor (p) through strong-good edges, then

[  
h_{q_1}\equiv h_{q_2}\pmod p.  
]

If (p\le B), this does not force equality. But if (q_1,q_2) labels are bounded by (B), congruence mod many different low primes (p) will force equality: if

[  
h_{q_1}\ne h_{q_2},  
]

then their difference (d) is a nonzero integer with

[  
|d|\le2B.  
]

It can be divisible by at most

[  
O(\log B/\log X)  
]

low primes (p\asymp X). But an expander gives many shared low neighbors. If more than that many shared primes impose congruence, then (d=0).

This is excellent! We do not need (p>B); many low primes together force equality.

Thus agreement propagation can use **multiple small moduli** whose product exceeds (B).

---

## 9. Product-of-moduli comparison

If high labels (h_{q_1},h_{q_2}) satisfy

[  
h_{q_1}\equiv h_{q_2}\pmod p  
]

for many distinct low primes (p\in P), then

[  
\prod p\mid h_{q_1}-h_{q_2}.  
]

But

[  
|h_{q_1}-h_{q_2}|\le 2B.  
]

If product exceeds (2B), difference must be zero.

Since each (p\asymp X), it suffices to have

[  
s>\frac{\log(2B)}{\log X}  
]

shared low neighbors. With

[  
B\asymp k_0X,  
]

this number is about

[  
1+o(1)  
]

or at most (2). Indeed (X^2\gg B). So two distinct low primes already force equality for large (X).

This is very strong.

Thus if two high vertices share at least two strong-good low neighbors, their labels equal.

---

## 10. Agreement graph on high vertices

Construct an auxiliary graph (\Gamma) on high vertices (Q):

connect (q_1,q_2) if they have at least two common low neighbors (p) such that both incident edges are strong-good.

Then labels (h_q) are constant on connected components of (\Gamma).

If the original bipartite test graph is a good expander and most edges are strong-good, then (\Gamma) should have one giant component containing most high vertices.

Therefore most high vertices share one common label (h). Then most low vertices connected by strong-good edges to this giant component also have residues congruent to (h). Thus global agreement.

This is a very concrete mechanism!

---

## 11. Energy–entropy conclusion

If strong-good edges are dense, agreement propagation gives global (h) on most vertices.

If strong-good edges are not dense, many edges are bad, each pays energy at least

[  
(B/(pq))^2.  
]

Thus either:

1. energy large; or
    
2. assignment close to global h.
    

This is exactly the desired Poincaré dichotomy.

This is much cleaner than abstract entropy.

---

## 12. What remains to ensure?

We need a bipartite graph design with:

1. low degree about (C\log p) perhaps;
    
2. high vertices share enough common low neighbors so (\Gamma) connects;
    
3. expansion robust after deleting an (\eta) fraction of bad edges.
    

Random bipartite graphs with degree (d=C\log N) usually have good expansion, but two high vertices sharing two neighbors requires common neighborhoods. In sparse random bipartite graph with left size (N_L), right size (N_R), degree (d), expected common neighbors between two right vertices is roughly

[  
N_L\left(\frac{d}{N_R}\right)^2.  
]

If (N_L\sim N_R\sim N), this is (d^2/N), tiny. So sparse random graph high vertices usually do **not** share two neighbors.

Thus common-neighbor propagation needs denser graph or a designed block structure.

This is important.

## 13. Need use complete or semi-dense blocks for agreement

For high vertices to share low neighbors, the bipartite graph must be much denser than (O(\log p))-degree random graph.

Complete bipartite block has abundant common neighbors. Candidate graph originally may indeed include complete block pairs; Fourier random edge selection uses all candidate edges. We do not need test graph to be sparse if using it for agreement; full candidate graph has huge edge set.

But total mass? Full compatible block graph was our candidate set, with total mass tuned by choosing K. It includes complete block pairs. So for agreement, we can use full block pairs, not sparse tests.

This simplifies: use complete or dense (P_k\times P_{1.5k}) block pairs as comparison gadgets. They have abundant common neighbors.

Fourier coefficient product includes all these edges, so bad edge energy accumulates over complete block. Great.

## 14. Return to full dyadic block graph

Thus the correct picture:

- Candidate graph includes complete bipartite pairs for compatible dyadic blocks.
    
- Agreement propagation uses the density of complete block pairs.
    
- No need sparse (O(\log p)) test graph for proof, though entropy intuition remains.
    
- Total candidate mass is controlled by choosing finite scale range (k_0\le k\le K); complete compatible block graph over that range has mass (\asymp\log(K/k_0)), tunable.
    

This is much better. Earlier we worried full graph too many edges; but we can tune K and θ. With (W\asymp2/b), θ≈1/2, no problem.

So the agreement theorem may be easier than feared because graph is dense.

## 15. Updated route

Use full dyadic compatible block graph. For each adjacent scale pair (P_k,Q_{1.5k}):

- If many edges are strong-good, complete bipartite structure forces common label (h) across blocks.
    
- If not, many bad edges contribute energy.
    
- Scale graph connectivity propagates one h across all blocks.
    

This becomes a combinatorial robust agreement problem on dense block pairs, not sparse expander.

This is a significant improvement.
