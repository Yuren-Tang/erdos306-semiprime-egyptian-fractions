好。現在分析 **above-top label regime**，也就是：

[  
h  
]

大到超過我們可用最高尺度的可見範圍時，是否仍可能有小能量

[  
E(h)=\sum_{pq\in E}\left|\frac{h}{pq}\right|^2.  
]

這是 finite-scale proof 的「封頂問題」。

---

# 1. 問題形態

對 diagonal character

[  
a_p\equiv h\pmod p,  
]

邊 (pq) 的相位是

[  
\frac{h}{pq}\pmod1.  
]

能量是

[  
E(h)=\sum_{pq\in E}\left|\frac{h}{pq}\right|^2.  
]

若 (h) 是小整數，且

[  
|h|<\min pq/2,  
]

則

[  
E(h)=h^2\sum_{pq}\frac1{p^2q^2}.  
]

這是 Gaussian 區。

困難是 (h) 很大時，可能對許多 (pq)

[  
h\equiv 0\pmod{pq}  
]

或接近整數倍，使

[  
\left|\frac{h}{pq}\right|  
]

很小。

要證 high frequency tail，就要說：除非 (h) 在 CRT 意義下其實等於小 label，否則它不可能同時對很多 semiprime moduli (pq) 都接近 (0).

---

# 2. exact divisibility case

先看最簡情形：

[  
pq\mid h.  
]

若 (h) 被許多 semiprimes (pq) 整除，則 (h) 必含有許多 prime factors from (\mathcal P).

設

[  
S(h)={p\in\mathcal P:p\mid h}.  
]

則

[  
pq\mid h  
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad  
p,q\in S(h)  
]

對 squarefree (pq) 而言。

若 (S(h)) 只佔少數 primes，則絕大多數 edges 不會有 exact zero phase。

若 (S(h)) 佔大多數 primes，則 (h) 被大多數 primes 的乘積整除，因而

[  
|h|  
\ge  
\prod_{p\in S(h)}p.  
]

在 modulo (L) 的對稱代表區間

[  
|h|\le L/2,  
]

這仍可能發生，如果 (S(h)) 不是全部 primes。但這種 (h) 的數量與 complement primes 的選擇有關；其 entropy 應由 edges incident to complement 支付。

所以 exact divisibility 本身不會形成大 obstruction。

---

# 3. approximate divisibility 更難

問題是

[  
\left|\frac{h}{pq}\right|\ll1  
]

不要求 (pq\mid h)，只要求 (h) 接近某個 multiple of (pq).

對固定 (h)，這是說

[  
h\bmod pq  
]

落在短 interval near (0).

若 (p,q) 像隨機模數，則 (h\bmod pq) 應分散，很難對大量 (pq) 同時接近 (0). 但要證這一點，需要一種 divisor/reciprocal distribution estimate。

這和 one-dimensional aperiodicity 問題本質相同。

---

# 4. 可能用 Erdős–Turán / divisor bound？

對高頻 (h)，考慮固定 (p)，隨 (q) 變化：

# [  
\left|\frac{h}{pq}\right|

\left|\frac{h\bar p}{q}\right|?  
]

小心：(\frac{h}{pq}) 是實數，不是 modulo prime (q) 的 inverse expression。若 (h) 是整數，(|h/(pq)|) 等於 (h\bmod pq) 除以 (pq).  
固定 (p)，令

[  
h=p u+r.  
]

模 (pq) 的行為跟 (q\mid h-r') 相關，不像 Irving 的 (\bar p/q) 形式。

所以 Irving 不直接控制 diagonal high frequency。這是一個獨立的一維 lattice aperiodicity problem。

---

# 5. 可以靠一般 Bernoulli local CLT 嗎？

這裏或許應該回到一般 weighted Bernoulli local CLT。對角部分本來是一維 sum：

[  
S=\sum_{pq}X_{pq}\frac1{pq}.  
]

整數化 weights：

[  
w_{pq}=L/(pq).  
]

Characteristic function high frequency 就是

[  
\prod_{pq}  
(1-\theta+\theta e(hw_{pq}/L)).  
]

一般 local CLT 對 triangular arrays 的 aperiodicity 要求是：

[  
\sup_{\delta\le |t|\le \pi}  
\prod_i|\varphi_i(t)|  
\to0  
]

或某種 maximal span condition。

我們的 weights (L/(pq)) 有強 CRT 結構，span 1，且很多 weights。可能已有標準 theorem 可用，但要驗證 aperiodicity。

這可能比自行處理 approximate divisibility easier。

---

# 6. Span 1 不夠，但我們有很多 small perturbing weights

一個常見 sufficient condition 是：對每個 modulus (m), weights 不都集中在同一 residue class mod (m)，且 variance from weights not divisible by (m) is large. Our weights

[  
w_{pq}=L/(pq)  
]

have the property:

For a prime (r\mid L), (w_{pq}) is not divisible by (r) iff (r=p) or (r=q). Thus many edges incident to (r) provide weights nonzero mod (r).

This suggests strong aperiodicity.

For composite moduli (d\mid L), weights not divisible by (d) are edges involving primes from (d)'s support. There are many unless (d) has huge support. If (d) has huge support, then its modulus is enormous and frequency neighborhoods are small.

This can probably be made into a CRT aperiodicity lemma.

---

# 7. Diagonal high-frequency as subset support

Let

[  
h\bmod L.  
]

Define support of (h)'s divisibility:

[  
T={p\in\mathcal P: h\equiv0\pmod p}.  
]

If (h) is nonzero modulo many primes, then edges incident to those primes produce nontrivial phases.

More generally, for each prime (p), (h\bmod p) is nonzero or zero. The diagonal character restricted to vertex (p) is (h_p=h\bmod p).

Then diagonal high-frequency is not special: it is a character assignment. The agreement theorem should control it too. If (h) has many nonzero residues, vertex correction / agreement sees it. If it has zero residues on most primes and nonzero on few, its entropy is small and incident edges to nonzero primes pay.

So perhaps above-top diagonal can be handled by the same finite-scale weighted CRT agreement theorem once proved. We do not need separate aperiodicity. But to prove the theorem we cannot rely on it circularly.

---

# 8. Maybe central theorem should include diagonal high frequency

Yes: the central counting inequality

[  
#{a:Q(a)\le R}  
\le  
e^{\varepsilon R}  
#{h:Q_{\mathrm{diag}}(h)\le CR}  
]

does not require us to bound diagonal (h) inside the agreement theorem. It leaves all diagonal residues (h\bmod L) on the right. Then one-dimensional diagonal tail is handled separately by local CLT/aperiodicity.

Thus above-top issue belongs to diagonal tail, not agreement.

So we need either:

1. prove one-dimensional aperiodicity for weights (1/(pq)); or
    
2. keep diagonal high h in right side and show its contribution is small using another theorem.
    

This is more modular.

---

# 9. How to prove diagonal tail?

Maybe use known results on Egyptian fraction random sums? There may be local CLT for random subset sums with divisibility-rich weights. But our weights are special.

A sufficient direct approach:

For any nonzero (h\bmod L), let

[  
S={p:h\not\equiv0\pmod p}.  
]

If (S) nonempty, choose (p\in S). For edges (pq) with many q, phase

[  
\frac{h}{pq}\pmod1  
]

viewed via CRT equals?

Since (h\bmod p\ne0), the residue of (h) modulo (pq) is not divisible by p. But its size modulo (pq) could still be small? If (h\bmod pq=r) with (|r|\ll pq), and (r\bmod p=h\bmod p\ne0). There are many possible small r congruent to h mod p; about (B/p) within ([-B,B]). For fixed p, varying q, can h mod pq be small often?

This relates to distribution of multiples of q near h with congruence. Could use divisor bounds: if (|h-m pq|\le B), then q divides approximately ((h-r)/p). Number of q satisfying small remainder maybe bounded by divisor function over interval.

For many q in a block, being near multiple of pq imposes q divides one of (O(B/p)) integers near h/p. The number of prime divisors across these integers may be bounded by (O((B/p)\log h)). If block has many q, choose B small enough, get nonconcentration.

This may yield diagonal aperiodicity by elementary divisor counting.

---

# 10. Sketch divisor bound

Fix p with h mod p = r_p nonzero. Suppose for q in Q,

[  
\left|\frac{h}{pq}\right|<\delta  
]

means there exists integer m such that

[  
|h-mpq|<\delta pq.  
]

Modulo p, this implies

[  
h-mpq\equiv h\equiv r_p\pmod p,  
]

not simplifying since (pq\equiv0\mod p). Let n=h-mpq. Then n is a small integer (|n|<\delta pq) congruent r_p mod p, and q divides h-n divided by p? Since h-n=mpq, yes q | (h-n)/p.

For each possible n in interval length (\delta pq\sim \delta pQ), q divides (h-n)/p. Number of n is (\sim \delta Q) for each residue mod p. This could be large enough to cover many q if (\delta) fixed. Not good.

But energy requires (\delta) maybe small; for high-frequency tail need constant fraction away from zero. This divisor bound may be weak.

---

# 11. Maybe use many p simultaneously

For h to have small phase for many edges (pq), for many p and q. Even if for each p there are some q, complete bipartite density may fail. Dense agreement again says if many edges have small h mod pq, then there is small label (n) congruent to h mod many p,q, forcing h equal small mod product. This is exact our bounded agreement with diagonal residues.

Thus diagonal aperiodicity can be proved by dense agreement: if many edges have small phase at cutoff B, then h has a small representative on many primes; if repeated over enough blocks, h is small modulo large product; otherwise many edges have phase >B/(pq), giving energy.

So central theorem and diagonal tail are intertwined but not circular if we prove dense agreement directly for diagonal h.

This returns to multiscale agreement.

---

# 12. Current best modularization

Maybe final proof should not separate diagonal high tail at all. Instead prove directly:

[  
\sum_{a\notin\mathfrak M}e^{-cQ(a)}=o(1/\sigma)  
]

using the weighted CRT agreement theorem, whose right side only counts small major h. That theorem must include diagonal high h as non-major.

This is stronger than previous counting with all diagonal h on right.

But then the central theorem is exactly the whole minor arc.

We have circled.

---

# 13. Honest state

Above-top diagonal/aperiodicity is another real obstacle. It is likely solvable by the same multiscale dense agreement machinery, but we have not written a clean proof.

So the remaining hard core is actually two linked things:

1. weighted CRT list-agreement for non-diagonal characters;
    
2. diagonal high-frequency aperiodicity.
    

Both are manifestations of the same principle: if many CRT glues are small, there is a small global h.

This suggests the ultimate central theorem should be:

[  
\boxed{  
\text{If }Q(a)\text{ is small, then }a\text{ is close to a small diagonal }h.  
}  
]

not just close to some diagonal modulo L.

That is the real Poincaré inequality.

---

# 14. Final central theorem: CRT Poincaré inequality

We need:

[  
Q(a)  
\ge  
c, d(a,\mathfrak M)^2  
]

in an entropy/counting sense, where (\mathfrak M) is small diagonal arc.

More practically:

For all (R),

[  
#{a:Q(a)\le R,\ a\notin\mathfrak M}  
\le  
e^{\varepsilon R}\cdot o(1/\sigma)  
]

or the Fourier sum version directly.

This theorem includes all high-frequency and non-diagonal cases.

---

# 15. What next?

At this point, further progress likely requires developing this CRT Poincaré theorem carefully, not ad hoc. The next manageable test is to prove it for **one complete bipartite block pair plus a size cutoff**, which we did; then for **a tower with no top boundary** maybe by imposing boundary condition; but finite full proof remains open.

Given the time, the honest next step is to summarise the current state and identify the exact open lemma, rather than pretend to close it.

But if we continue mathematically, I would next attempt a direct statement:

**Small-glue density theorem.**  
If for a character (a), a positive fraction of edges in every sufficiently high block pair have

[  
|H_{pq}^{*}|\le B_{pq}  
]

with (B_{pq}) at the Gaussian scale, then (a) is major arc.

This may be provable via iterative dense agreement.

Shall proceed with that as next direction.
