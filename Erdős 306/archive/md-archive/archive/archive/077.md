好。現在精算 **exception vertices 的 entropy 是否由 bad-edge energy 支付**。這是 backbone agreement proposition 的最後一個關鍵缺口。

---

## 1. 單個 dense block pair 的例外頂點

固定一個 upward backbone block pair：

[  
P=P_k,\qquad Q=P_{3k/2}.  
]

記

[  
X=2^k,\qquad Y=X^{3/2},  
]

並取 cutoff

[  
B=X^{1+\eta},\qquad \frac14<\eta<\frac12.  
]

邊 (pq) strong-good 若

[  
|H_{pq}^{*}|\le B.  
]

若 bad edge 密度為

[  
\varepsilon,  
]

dense block agreement lemma 給出 label

[  
h,\qquad |h|\le B,  
]

使得除了 (O(\sqrt\varepsilon)) 比例外，大多數 (p\in P)、(q\in Q) 被 (h) 解釋。

更精確地，若一個高點 (q\in Q) 的 strong-good degree 至少

[  
(1-\sqrt\varepsilon)|P|,  
]

則它有唯一 label (h_q)。大多數 (q) 是這樣的，而且所有這些 (h_q) 相等，稱為 (h)。

例外高點數量

[  
\ll \sqrt\varepsilon |Q|.  
]

低點例外也類似，數量

[  
\ll \sqrt\varepsilon |P|.  
]

---

## 2. 例外頂點的 entropy

若某個低點 (p) 沒有被 label (h) 控制，最粗略地，它的 residue (a_p) 有

[  
p\asymp X  
]

種可能，entropy 約

[  
\log X\asymp k.  
]

若例外低點比例為

[  
\delta_P\ll \sqrt\varepsilon,  
]

則低側例外 entropy 至多

# [  
\delta_P |P|\log X  
\asymp  
\sqrt\varepsilon\cdot \frac{X}{k}\cdot k

\sqrt\varepsilon,X.  
]

高側例外 entropy 至多

# [  
\sqrt\varepsilon |Q|\log Y  
\asymp  
\sqrt\varepsilon\cdot \frac{Y}{\ell}\cdot \ell

\sqrt\varepsilon,Y.  
]

因為

[  
Y=X^{3/2},  
]

高側例外 entropy 佔主導：

[  
\asymp \sqrt\varepsilon,X^{3/2}.  
]

這看起來很大。

但這只是單一 block pair 的粗糙 entropy。如果每個高點也是下一層 block 的低點，它還會被下一個 agreement pair 控制。真正自由的例外頂點必須在多個相鄰 block pairs 中都逃脫，否則會被別的 pair label 住。

所以不能在單一 block pair 上孤立地把高側例外 entropy 全算成自由。需要沿 backbone 累計。

---

## 3. 尺度鏈上的例外傳播

每個 block (P_k) 出現在兩種角色中：

1. 作為低層，參與
    

[  
P_k\leftrightarrow P_{3k/2};  
]

2. 作為高層，參與
    

[  
P_{2k/3}\leftrightarrow P_k  
]

如果 (2k/3) 在尺度範圍內。

因此一個 block 的 vertices 只要在任一相鄰 good pair 中被 label 解釋，就不是自由的。只有當它周圍的 agreement edges 被 bad cuts 隔開，才產生真正自由 entropy。

這與前面「bad cuts 分割尺度圖」一致。

所以 exception entropy 應按尺度 components 計算，而不是按單一 pair 計算。

---

## 4. Component 內的例外

假設尺度 component 由一串 good pairs 連成：

[  
k_1\to k_2\to\cdots\to k_m.  
]

所有 good pairs 的 labels 已合併為同一

[  
h_C.  
]

在 component 內，一個頂點若在某個 incident good pair 中不是例外，就被迫滿足

[  
a_p\equiv h_C\pmod p.  
]

只有那些在所有 incident good pairs 中都是例外的頂點才有自由 residue。

對內部尺度 (k_i)，它有左右兩個 incident good pairs。要成為真正自由例外，它必須在兩邊都例外。若每邊例外比例約 (\sqrt\varepsilon)，兩者交集比例通常應更小；即使不用獨立性，仍至多其中一邊例外比例。也就是

[  
O(\sqrt\varepsilon).  
]

這部分 entropy 可由相鄰 bad edge density支付。

但要精確閉合，需要避免 (\sqrt\varepsilon) 導致能量不足，因 energy 是 (\varepsilon T_k) 而 entropy 是 (\sqrt\varepsilon |P_k|\log X)。(\sqrt\varepsilon) 比 (\varepsilon) 大，可能壞。

所以 dense agreement lemma 的 (O(\sqrt\varepsilon)) 例外率太粗。需要改進到 (O(\varepsilon))。

---

## 5. 改善例外率：用 degree pruning

其實可以得到 (O(\varepsilon)) 類型的「edge exception」，但 vertex exception 用 Markov 自然是 (\sqrt\varepsilon)。可通過調參：

稱高點 (q) good 若 bad degree 至多

[  
\alpha |P|.  
]

則 bad 高點數至多

[  
\frac{\varepsilon}{\alpha}|Q|.  
]

對 good 高點，strong-good degree 至少

[  
(1-\alpha)|P|.  
]

兩個 good 高點的 common good neighbors 至少

[  
(1-2\alpha)|P|.  
]

只需

[  
\alpha<1/2  
]

即可有共同鄰居。也就是 (\alpha) 不必取 (\sqrt\varepsilon)，可以取固定小常數，例如 (0.01)。

那 bad 高點比例至多

[  
100\varepsilon.  
]

很好！例外 vertex 比例是 (O(\varepsilon))，不是 (O(\sqrt\varepsilon))。

同理低點也可用固定 threshold 得到 (O(\varepsilon)) 例外比例。

這是重要修正。

---

## 6. Dense agreement lemma 的強版本

若 bad edge 密度為 (\varepsilon), 且 (\varepsilon) 足夠小，則存在 label (h) 使得除了

[  
O(\varepsilon)  
]

比例的 vertices 外，皆由 (h) 解釋。

證明：

- 稱 (q) regular 若 bad degree (\le \alpha |P|)。不 regular 的 (q) 數 (\le \varepsilon |Q|/\alpha).
    
- 任兩 regular (q_1,q_2) 的 good-neighbor intersection 至少 ((1-2\alpha)|P|)，包含兩個以上 primes，故 labels equal.
    
- 因此所有 regular (q) label 相同。
    
- 稱 (p) regular 若它對 regular (Q)-vertices 有至少一條 strong-good edge；不然它會造成 (\gg |Q|) bad edges, 故數量 (O(\varepsilon)|P|).
    
- 這些 regular (p) 也被同一 h 解釋。
    

所以 vertex exception (O(\varepsilon)) 成立。

很好。

---

## 7. 例外 entropy 重新估計

單 block pair 中，例外低點 entropy：

[  
\ll \varepsilon |P|\log X  
\asymp \varepsilon X.  
]

例外高點 entropy：

[  
\ll \varepsilon |Q|\log Y  
\asymp \varepsilon Y.  
]

但 energy lower bound for bad density (\varepsilon) was

# [  
E_{\text{bad}}  
\gg  
\varepsilon T_k

\varepsilon\frac{X^{2\eta-1/2}}{k^2}.  
]

This is still much smaller than (\varepsilon Y=\varepsilon X^{3/2}). So energy does **not** pay raw vertex entropy of arbitrary exception residues.

This looks dangerous.

But note: Fourier energy (Q) is dimensionless; entropy counts characters. A single block pair's bad energy threshold (T_k) is not meant to pay the entropy of all exception vertices in that huge block. If a positive fraction of high vertices are completely free, there are (e^{\varepsilon Y}) assignments, far too many compared to energy (e^{-c\varepsilon T_k}). Since (T_k\ll Y), this would fail.

Therefore we cannot allow exception vertices to be arbitrary. They must be controlled by other full graph/backbone edges. The single pair lemma alone insufficient for entropy.

This is important.

---

## 8. Need use full edge product for exception vertices

If a high vertex (q) is exceptional in pair (P_k\leftrightarrow P_{1.5k}), it is still connected to many other blocks in full graph/backbone:

- downward from other lower scales;
    
- upward to (P_{2.25k});
    
- lateral compatible pairs in full graph.
    

If its residue is arbitrary, many incident edges in those other pairs will have large phase, contributing much more energy.

Thus exception entropy is not paid by one pair's bad edge energy, but by the total incident energy across the full compatible graph.

This is where full graph, not just backbone, matters.

We need an **incident energy lemma**:

For a vertex (q), once a global label (h) is established in surrounding blocks, choosing

[  
a_q\not\equiv h\pmod q  
]

causes many incident edges to have phase spread, hence energy large enough to pay (\log q) entropy.

This is where Irving / reciprocal distribution enters naturally.

---

## 9. Vertex correction lemma

Suppose a large neighboring block (P) is already labelled by (h):

[  
a_p\equiv h\pmod p  
]

for most (p\in P).

Take a vertex (q) with residue

[  
a_q\ne h\pmod q.  
]

For edge (pq), phase equals

[  
\frac{h q^{-1}}p  
+  
\frac{a_q p^{-1}}q.  
]

The diagonal h phase would be

[  
\frac{h}{pq}.  
]

Subtracting, the main discrepancy is

[  
\frac{(a_q-h)p^{-1}}q.  
]

As (p) varies in a good prime block, (p^{-1}\bmod q) is distributed. If

[  
a_q-h\not\equiv0\pmod q,  
]

then Irving gives that for many (p), this term is away from integers. Hence incident energy over (p\in P) is large.

Quantitatively:

[  
\sum_{p\in P}  
\left|  
\frac{(a_q-h)p^{-1}}q  
\right|^2  
\gg |P|  
]

or at least (\gg |P|) up to constants.

Then Fourier energy for q's wrong residue is enormous, easily paying (\log q).

This is excellent. It means exception vertices are controlled by Irving, not by block agreement alone.

---

## 10. Division of roles refined

Now the full architecture is:

1. **Dense block agreement** gives a global label (h) on most vertices of each good scale component.
    
2. **Irving incident dispersion** forces remaining vertices to adopt that label, because any wrong residue creates large energy over incident edges to labelled blocks.
    
3. **Bad scale cuts** cost energy and allow multiple labels; their entropy is paid by cut energy.
    
4. Diagonal h handled by local CLT.
    

This is finally coherent.

---

## 11. Check incident energy magnitude

Let (q\sim Q), neighboring labelled block (P) has primes (p\sim X), with (X) in Irving range relative to Q.

For wrong residue

[  
d=a_q-h\not\equiv0\pmod q,  
]

Irving-type dispersion should imply many p satisfy

[  
\left|\frac{d p^{-1}}q\right|>\delta  
]

for fixed δ. Therefore each such edge Fourier factor gets constant energy. The number of p is

[  
|P|\asymp \frac{X}{\log X}.  
]

This is far larger than

[  
\log q\asymp \log Q.  
]

So wrong residue entropy is overwhelmingly paid.

This solves the exception entropy problem strongly.

---

## 12. Good: where Irving really belongs

This clarifies Irving's role:

- Dense CRT agreement is elementary and identifies labels.
    
- Irving is not mainly for pairwise label merging; it kills vertices or characters deviating from the established label.
    

This is natural and strong.

---

## 13. Updated backbone proposition

We should state it with two components:

**Backbone + Irving correction proposition.**  
If a character has non-negligible Fourier coefficient, then after deleting scale cuts whose cost is paid by energy, there is a label (h_C) on each scale component such that all but energy-paid vertices satisfy

[  
a_p\equiv h_C\pmod p.  
]

Wrong vertices pay incident Irving energy (\gg) their entropy.

Thus low-energy characters are essentially piecewise diagonal; pieces separated by costly cuts. Hence entropy-energy bound follows.

---

## 14. This is now very plausible

The earlier entropy obstruction from exception vertices is resolved by noticing they are not free: full graph incident edges to labelled blocks punish them massively.

So agreement proof uses:

- dense block CRT for coarse labels;
    
- Irving for pointwise correction;
    
- energy for cuts.
    

This is a robust triad.
